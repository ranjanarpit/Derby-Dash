Meet the Athletes: Horse Breeds in Racing

Colt

Definition: A colt is a young male horse typically under four years of age. Once a colt reaches maturity, it is referred to as a stallion if left intact or a gelding if castrated.

Characteristics: Colts are known for their energetic and playful behavior. They are in the process of growing and developing both physically and mentally. Colts require proper training and handling to ensure they mature into well-behaved adult horses.

Age: Under four years old.

Number of Races: Colts often start their racing careers at two years old and can participate in numerous races during their early years. On average, a colt might run in 5-10 races before reaching four years of age, depending on their training and health.

Usage: Colts are often trained for various equestrian disciplines, including racing, show jumping, and dressage, depending on their breed and lineage.
