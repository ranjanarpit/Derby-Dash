Meet the Athletes: Horse Breeds in Racing

Rig

Definition: A rig, also known as a ridgling or ridgeling, is a male horse with one or both testicles undescended, often mistaken for a gelding or a stallion.

Characteristics: Rigs may exhibit stallion-like behavior due to retained testicles that produce hormones. This condition can sometimes cause behavioral issues or complications. Veterinary intervention may be required to address this condition.

Age: Any age.

Number of Races: The number of races a rig can participate in varies widely and is dependent on their individual health and behavior management. There is no specific average number for this category.

Usage: Rigs can participate in most equestrian activities. However, their behavior may need careful management depending on their hormonal influences.
